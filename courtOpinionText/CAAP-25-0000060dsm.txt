 (1) On May 22, 2026, the court granted relief from 
default of the opening brief for a third time in this appeal, 
and an eleventh extension of time for the opening brief to June 1, 2026, with no further extensions absent extraordinary circumstances. The court cautioned Coward for a third time that any further default of the opening brief may result in sanctions 
authorized by Hawaiʻ i Rules of Appellate Procedure (HRAP) 
Rule 30, including, without limitation, monetary sanctions, the 
appeal being dismissed, or both; 
(2) In the Motion, Coward seeks a twelfth extension of 
time for the opening brief because the "fly leaf should have included 'Opening Brief.'" Coward states he expects to complete 
the correction by June 2, 2026; 
(3) Coward filed the Motion on June 1, 2026, and is in 
default of the opening brief for a fourth time in this appeal 
See HRAP Rule 29(b) ("The submission of a . . . motion for extension does not toll the time for filing a brief."). Coward fails to demonstrate extraordinary circumstances for another extension of time to file the opening brief and has not taken any further action in this appeal. 
Therefore, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED that the Motion is 
denied and the appeal is dismissed. HRAP Rule 30.
