A writ of mandamus or prohibition is also not intended to 
be used in lieu of normal appellate procedures.  See Kema, 91 
Hawaiʻi at 204, 982 P.2d at 338; Takao, 59 Haw. at 241, 580 P.2d 
at 62.  Petitioner had alternative means to seek relief by 
pursuing an appeal.  See Hawaiʻ i Revised Statutes § 641-1(a); 
Hawaiʻi Rules of Appellate Procedure (HRAP) Rule 4(a)(1).   
 It is ordered that the petition is denied. 
 
It is further ordered that the motion for emergency stay is 
denied.  See District Court Rules of Civil Procedure Rule 62; 
HRAP Rule 8; Kelepolo v. Fernandez, 148 Hawaiʻ i 182, 192 & n.19, 
468 P.3d 196, 206 & n.19 (2020).
